    Definition:

    Binary numbers use base 2, while decimal numbers use base 10. To convert binary to decimal, we adjust the base.

    Binary to Decimal

    Step 1: Multiply each digit of the Binary number with the place value of that digit, starting from

    right to left i.e. from LSB to MSB


    Step 2: Add the result of this multiplication and the decimal number will be formed.

    Binary Digit Power of two Result
    1 $$2^0 = 1$$ 1
    0 \[ 2^1 = 2 \] 2
    1 \[ 2^2 = 4 \] 4
    1 \[ 2^3 = 8 \] 8

    Sum all the results: 1 + 2 + 0 + 8 = 11

    Binary to Octal Conversion

    Step 1: Split the binary number into groups of three digits, starting from right to left (LSB to MSB)

    Step 2: Convert these groups into equivalent octal digits.

    Let's convert the binary number 10111010 to octal:

    Binary Group Octal Equivalent
    1 1
    011 3
    101 5
    0 0

    Binary Addition

    is similar to decimal addition, but it's done in base 2 instead of base 10.

    Let's add two binary numbers: 1011 and 1101.


    \begin{array}{r} \phantom{0}1011 \\ +1101 \\ \hline \end{array}

    Starting from the rightmost position:


    \begin{array}{rl} \phantom{00}1 & \text{(carry over 1)} \\ \phantom{0}1011 \\ +1101 \\ \hline \phantom{0}11000 & \text{(1 + 1 = 10, carry over 1)} \\ \end{array}


    \begin{array}{r} \phantom{0}1011 \\ +1101 \\ \hline \phantom{0}110 & \text{(first intermediate result)} \\ 110 & \text{(second intermediate result)} \\ \hline \phantom{0}11000 & \text{(final result)} \\ \end{array}

    Binary Substraction

    similar to decimal subtraction, we subtract the digits of the second number from the corresponding digits of the first number.

    Let's substract two binary numbers: 1011 and 1101.


    \begin{array}{r} \phantom{0}1101 \\ -\phantom{0}101 \\ \hline \phantom{0}1000 \\ \end{array}

    Starting from the rightmost position:


    \begin{array}{r} \phantom{0}1101 \\ -\phantom{0}101 \\ \hline \phantom{000}0 & \text{(1 - 1 = 0)} \\ \phantom{00}11 & \text{(0 - 0 = 0)} \\ \phantom{0}01 & \text{(1 - 1 = 0, borrow 1)} \\ \hline \phantom{0}1000 & \text{(final result)} \\ \end{array}

    Binary Multiplication

    similar to decimal subtraction, we subtract the digits of the second number from the corresponding digits of the first number.

    Let's multiply two binary numbers: 1011 and 1101.


    $$\require{enclose} \begin{array}{r@{\hspace{0.5em}}l} 1011 & \\ \underline{\times1101} & \\ 1011 & \quad \text{(Multiplying by 1)} & \\ 0000\phantom{1} & \quad \text{(Multiplying by 0, shifted one position to the left)}& \\ 1011\phantom{00} & \quad \text{(Multiplying by 1, shifted two positions to the left)} & \\ \underline{1011\phantom{11}} & \quad \text{(Multiplying by 1, shifted three positions to the left)} & \\ 10011011 & \text{(Final result)} & \\ \end{array}$$

    Binary Division

    divides the dividend by the divisor to obtain the quotient and possibly a remainder

    Divide as if both numbers are whole numbers.

    $$\require{enclose} \begin{array}{r@{\hspace{0.5em}}l} 10101 & \\ 101 \enclose{longdiv}{11001} & \\ \underline{-101}\phantom{10} & \quad \text{ (Subtracting the divisor from the dividend)}& \\ 100 & \\ \underline{-101} & \\ 1 & \quad \text{ (Final remainder is smaller than the divisor)}& \\ \end{array}$$
